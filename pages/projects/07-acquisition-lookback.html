OP-007 // ANALYTICS

Acquisition Lookback

LIVE 2025
SQL Python Cohort Analysis Analytics
Overview

A retrospective analysis of customer acquisition channels examining cohort retention, cost-per-acquisition by source, and lifetime value projections. The goal was to surface which channels drove durable, long-term growth versus short-term volume spikes — and to inform future channel budget allocation.

Problem

What Needed Solving

Acquisition spend was being evaluated primarily on volume metrics — signups, installs, first-session counts. This created a blind spot: channels that drove high initial volume but poor retention were being over-funded, while channels that acquired fewer but stickier users were underweighted.

  • No cohort-level view of retention by acquisition channel.
  • CAC calculations didn't account for downstream churn differences.
  • LTV estimates were global averages, not segmented by source.
Solution

How It Was Built

Users were grouped into monthly acquisition cohorts and tagged with their first-touch channel. Retention curves were built for each cohort × channel combination, tracking activity at 7-, 30-, 60-, and 90-day marks. CAC was recomputed as channel spend divided by retained users at 30 days — a more meaningful denominator than raw signups.

LTV projections were modeled by fitting a retention curve per channel and integrating expected revenue over a 12-month horizon. Results were packaged into a summary report comparing channels on adjusted CAC, 90-day retention rate, and projected 12-month LTV.

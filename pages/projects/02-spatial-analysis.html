OP-002 // ANALYTICS

Spatial Analysis of NYC Bakeries

LIVE 2024.02
Analytics Data Visualization Statistics
Overview

One of my first solo analytics projects, completed as a contractor for Chengu. The report uncovers entrepreneurial opportunities in NYC's pastry and bakery restaurant sector, assessing market viability and risk to help the client make an informed decision on where and what type of restaurant to open.

/// Github Repository - Analysis of NYC's Dessert Sector
Problem

What needed solving

New York is often crowned as the most diverse place on earth. With about 200,000 small businesses operating in New York City, it is one of the most competitive arenas for entrepreneurs in the United States. In this competitive space, entrepreneurs often question the viability in launching new ventures in NYC. In this exploratory analysis, I aim to identify potential opportunities for bakery and dessert businesses in NYC, using data from Yelp's developer API.

Stakeholder Questions

  • What is the viability of opening a dessert shop in NYC?
  • Are there areas more likely to see success than others?
  • What kind of pastries should I avoid selling?
Solution

How it was built

Business data was aggregated from Yelp's API using keywords associated with desserts (e.g. bake, cake, desserts, donut, bakery, patisserie, viennoiserie). ETL principles were applied to clean the dataset and improve integrity. Discrete values were scaled for normality, keywords and symbols were binary encoded, and KNN imputation was used to fill missing information.

An exploratory and statistical analysis examined the distribution of specialty shops across the five boroughs. Using mean review count and ratings as a success proxy, ANOVA and Tukey's Range tests measured mean differences between business cohorts. The presence of a listed phone number was also analyzed as a potential signal of engagement. ANOVA and Tukey's Range tests were additionally applied across price points against rating and review metrics.

The 'success' metric was calculated by finding the normalized 50th percentile of review_counts and ratings. Businesses exceeding both were given the feature 'is_successful'. Chi2 was then performed to observe the likelihood of success of each business category.

Finally, using the unsupervised learning method DBSCAN, a geospatial clustering analysis was performed to observe which areas had the most successful businesses.

Limitations: this analysis cannot account for social media presence or the influence of viral trends on business success. It is also constrained by available data — population density and neighborhood demographics are not captured, and Yelp does not provide business age, which would be a stronger indicator of long-term viability.

Outcomes

Results

Although bakeries and desserts classified businesses did not meet expectations, this does not indicate that these business types are not viable. This might also suggest a call for better talent and products in these sectors. Cafes having higher mean differences and standardized residual values may suggest that coffee shops provide an entry point for new customers for different business categories. Given the high mean difference in the 'other' category, savory items may entice more customers as well.

We also see businesses in areas of Queens, Brooklyn and Manhattan performing better than others, suggesting population density or demographic has an impact on the likelihood of a business seeing success.

This analysis reveals that dessert-focused businesses in New York City face a complex landscape of different challenges. However, with a strategic business model and optimal location, they can significantly increase their chances of success. Within the dessert sector, bakeries account for 25% of businesses within this cohort.

Comparative analysis between dessert and bakery categories and cafes reveals interesting dynamics. Dessert and bakery have a low mean difference in ratings, indicating a narrower range in customer satisfaction, but typically score lower than cafes. Coffee shops in particular stand out with the highest review counts, suggesting that they are more favorable of this cohort.

Price sensitivity analysis revealed nuanced customer behavior. Premium establishments ($$$$) face the most scrutiny — shown by the large spread in mean differences — yet average the highest ratings across all four price tiers. Mid-range businesses ($$$) attract critical reviews, though not to the same degree. Budget-tier businesses ($ and $$) draw less scrutiny but also see lower foot traffic compared to their higher-priced counterparts. Significant differences in review counts between the $$$–$$$$ tier and the $–$$ tier merit further investigation.

Diving deeper into the success metrics, Chi-square analysis revealed meaningful distinctions. By comparing expected and actual results, this analysis yielded a Chi2 value of 84.46 with a p-value of 0.05, indicating a statistically significant difference between business categories. Contrary to what we have seen so far, bakeries were found to be less likely to achieve 'success' compared to random chance, indicated by its magnitude and direction in the 0 column. Conversely, ice cream shops, cafes and chocolate shops emerged as categories with a high chance of success.

DBSCAN spatial cluster analysis was then performed to cluster businesses that were labeled as 'successful'. Given the geographic sparsity of businesses in Queens and Brooklyn when compared to Manhattan, cluster distances were modified for each borough. Within the dessert sector, we see that the UES, Midtown and Tribeca has the highest density of 'successful' businesses. In Queens, clusters are more diffuse, particularly in Eastern Queens. LIC, Sunnyside, Astoria, Woodside show strong clustering, while Ridgewood, Forest Hills, Jamaica, Flushing and Bayside are more sparse. In Brooklyn, western Brooklyn seems to see more successful businesses, particularly in areas of Bushwick, Williamsburg, Redhook, and Fort Hamilton.

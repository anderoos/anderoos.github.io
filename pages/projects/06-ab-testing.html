OP-006 // ANALYTICS

AB Testing with MSU Library Website

LIVE 2024.02
A/B Testing Statistics Google Analytics UX Research
Overview

Montana State University's library Google Analytics data showed that the "Interact" tile card was consistently overlooked by users — accounting for only 2% of click-through rate despite providing access to essential resources like library hours, floor plans, and faculty contacts. Using A/B testing, this project evaluates alternative call-to-action labels to improve user engagement through controlled, data-driven decisions.

Methods

Design and Approach

Problem Definition

MSU Library click data was collected via CrazyEgg over one month, capturing 10,819 homepage visits. "Interact" comprised only 2% of total CTR compared to "Find" (35%) and "Request" (6%). The library committee hypothesized that the label was ineffective or confusing. Four alternative CTAs were proposed: "Connect", "Learn", "Help", and "Services".

Qualitative Research

Student interviews were conducted with sophomore, junior, and senior students to validate that the alternative labels were meaningfully different and to shape the hypothesis. Based on feedback, "Service" or "Help" were expected to be the most effective in driving engagement by providing clarity and reducing confusion.

Deploying the A/B Test

Data was collected over three weeks. Users were equally randomized across all five CTA variations using Google Analytics Experiments. CrazyEgg supplemented GA by capturing click behavior and user interaction patterns. Metrics tracked: click-through rate, bounce rate, and exit rate — to assess both attraction and post-click satisfaction.

  • Click-through rate: measures the CTA's ability to attract users.
  • Bounce rate: indicates users who did not find expected content and continued exploring.
  • Exit rate: implies users found what they needed and left the site.
